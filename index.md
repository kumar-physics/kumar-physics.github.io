# Welcom to my space


I am a structural biologist and data scientist passionate about applying data-driven approaches to advance biomolecular research. I currently serve as Lead Analyst at the Biological Magnetic Resonance Data Bank (BMRB) at UConn Health, where I lead NMR biocuration and validation efforts within the Worldwide Protein Data Bank (wwPDB) consortium. A core part of my work involves building tools and workflows that enable scalable, standardized, and FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable) data practices — principles that are foundational to data science and essential for preparing high-quality data for machine learning model training. I am also developing machine learning models that integrate information from the Protein Data Bank (wwPDB) and BMRB across multiple levels, including sequence, residue, and atom.

At BMRB, I have designed interactive data visualization tools and am currently developing comprehensive, automated validation reporting systems aligned with machine-learning–oriented metadata curation goals. I have also collaborated with research groups across the field to help establish reporting standards for NMR relaxation studies and NMR screening data, contributing to greater consistency and rigor in how these data types are documented and shared. My work spans the full data lifecycle — from schema design and ontology integration to longitudinal data tracking — all critical to reproducibility and effective ML model training. Throughout, I remain committed to advancing data-driven science through scalable infrastructure, robust modeling frameworks, and collaborative, interdisciplinary approaches.
